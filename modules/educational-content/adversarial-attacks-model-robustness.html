1. Overview

Adversarial attacks represent a sophisticated frontier in AI security. Unlike prompt injection, which manipulates the semantic output of a model through linguistic trickery, adversarial attacks target the structural and mathematical essence of machine learning systems.

Underlying Vulnerabilities

Models operate in high-dimensional continuous spaces. Attacks exploit vulnerabilities within architecture and weight distribution.

Model Robustness

A system's resilience against these underlying mathematical manipulations across all modalities (text, vision, audio).

02 Evasion Attacks

Inference-Stage Attacks occurring after deployment.

Adversarial Perturbations

These are meticulously calculated changes added to input data. While they often remain invisible to human perception, they are magnified by the linearity of neural networks in high-dimensional spaces, drastically altering the final activation and crossing decision boundaries.

White-Box Attack

FGSM

Fast Gradient Sign Method uses the gradient of the loss with respect to input data. It adds a small fraction (scaled by epsilon) to the original input to maximize loss quickly.

Iterative Attack

PGD

Projected Gradient Descent takes multiple smaller steps in the gradient direction, projecting the input back into a valid mathematical boundary (epsilon-ball) after each step.

Physical Evasion Case Study

Attacks are not limited to digital noise. Physical artifacts, such as specifically patterned tape on a "Stop" sign, can act as adversarial patches. This causes autonomous vehicle models to misclassify the sign, demonstrating real-world safety implications.

03 Poisoning Attacks

Training-Stage Attacks targeting the learning phase.

Targeted Poisoning (Backdoor/Trojan)

Attackers introduce a hidden "trigger" (like a specific pixel pattern) into training data and associate it with an incorrect label. The model learns normally but contains a silent vulnerability: it behaves correctly until it encounters that specific trigger at inference time.

Data Availability Attacks

Instead of a specific target, these aim to degrade the overall accuracy and performance of the model by injecting subtly corrupted data throughout the training set.

LLM Context Poisoning

Crucial for RAG systems. Attackers host web pages with invisible text or malicious structures. When a model scrapes or retrieves this data, it absorbs poisoned information into its weights or active context window.

04 Defense Strategies

Adversarial Training Most Direct

During training, adversarial examples (generated via PGD) are actively included in training batches with correct labels. This forces the model to learn correct decision boundaries even in the presence of mathematical noise.

Defensive Distillation

A "student" model is trained on the output probabilities (soft labels) of a larger "teacher" model. This smooths decision boundaries, making the network less sensitive to small gradient shifts.

Feature Squeezing

Sanitizing data before it reaches the inference engine by reducing complexity (e.g., color bit-depth reduction). This "squeezes" out imperceptible perturbations.

Module Summary

  • AI security encompasses structural, mathematical exploits in addition to logical manipulation.
  • Evasion attacks (FGSM, PGD) weaponize gradients for misclassification.
  • Poisoning attacks corrupt foundational data, embedding silent vulnerabilities.
